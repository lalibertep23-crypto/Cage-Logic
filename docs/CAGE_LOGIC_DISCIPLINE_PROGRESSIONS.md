# CAGE LOGIC — DISCIPLINE PROGRESSION SYSTEM
## Master Product Specification

---

## THE SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY

Every discipline in Cage Logic solves the same problem:
> "I don't know if I'm improving."

That's why white belts quit BJJ. That's why beginners drop out of every combat sport. Progress is invisible without a map.

Cage Logic makes it visible — in every discipline, in a way that is culturally authentic to that sport.

**The system is unified. The cultures are not.**

Each discipline must feel like it was built *inside* that sport — not imported from BJJ. A wrestler should look at the wrestling section and feel recognized. A Muay Thai fighter should see ritual, not a checklist. A boxer should feel the precision and patience of the Sweet Science.

**Universal structure:**
1. Weekly focus + drillable skill
2. Small win — the moment you feel it click
3. Milestone checklist — earned, not given
4. Coach signoff — authority and accountability
5. Identity shift — who you are now vs. who you were

**The five progression cultures:**
- BJJ: **sacred** — belt hierarchy, lineage, patience
- Wrestling: **forged** — pressure, chains, grinding domination
- Muay Thai: **ritualistic** — weapons, composure, repetition
- Boxing: **precise** — IQ, timing, composure under fire
- MMA: **tactical** — system integration, fight camp evolution

# DISCIPLINE 1: BJJ
## "Your journey. Earned. Not given."

### Cultural Identity

BJJ progression is sacred. The belt means something beyond sport — it carries lineage, sacrifice, and identity. No one outside the mat room can fake it. Black belts take 10 years. White belts quit in 90 days. The system exists to prevent that second thing.

The visual language: folded belts, cotton weave, warm brass light, worn mat texture. Every promotion photo looks like a ritual because it is one.

### Progression Tiers

| Belt | Stripes | Identity |
|------|---------|----------|
| WHITE | 0–4 | Survival |
| BLUE | 0–4 | Foundation |
| PURPLE | 0–4 | Dangerous |
| BROWN | 0–4 | Advanced |
| BLACK | 1st–5th Degree | Mastery |

### First 90 Days — White Belt (Iron Army Standard)

*Source: Iron Army Onboarding Packet V2. This is the built and validated content. Use it as the product standard for all other disciplines.*

| Weeks | Focus | Drill | Small Win |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|
| 1–2 | Survive on the bottom | Shrimp, technical stand-up, frames | Stop getting smashed in side control |
| 3–4 | Closed guard basics | Hold guard, break posture, hip bump sweep | Hold closed guard for a full round |
| 5–6 | Side control: top + bottom | Knee-cut pass / knee-in escape | Escape side control once per roll |
| 7–8 | Mount + back basics | Upa escape, back control, RNC mechanics | Maintain back control 30+ seconds |
| 9–10 | Standup — Iron Army DNA | Pummeling, underhooks, single-leg | Attempt a takedown in live rolling |
| 11–12 | Chain it together | Sweep → pass → control → submit | Link two positions in one roll |
| 13 | Stripe check-in | Journal review with coach | Name what got better and what's next |

### What Earns White Belt Stripe 1

- ☐ Demonstrate technical stand-up without thinking
- ☐ Escape side control to guard or knees
- ☐ Hold closed guard against a same-size training partner
- ☐ Finish a rear naked choke with proper mechanics
- ☐ 24+ classes logged (2x/week × 12 weeks)
- ☐ Tap a teammate in live rolling — even once
- ☐ Coach signoff

### App Experience Notes

The belt promotion moment is a ceremony. Full-screen reveal. Warm light. No confetti. No animations that cheapen it. The copy should feel like a coach handing you the belt, not a badge unlocking in a game.

# DISCIPLINE 2: WRESTLING
## "Forged. Not awarded."

### Cultural Identity

Wrestling has no ceremony. No belt. No handshake that changes your rank. You earn your position on every single mat. The culture is brutal, earned, and systemized — and it is deeply identity-driven in ways most sports are not.

A wrestler doesn't say "I do wrestling." They say "I'm a wrestler." That distinction matters. The app must reflect it.

Wrestling is about **where the fight goes**. Whoever controls position controls the outcome. That's not a technique — it's a philosophy. The entire progression system is built around that idea: you earn the right to dictate the fight.

The visual language is not belts. It is **chains** — literally. Chain wrestling is the core technical concept: one attack flows into the next. Forged chain links as a visual metaphor for progression is authentic to the sport and to Cage Logic's industrial aesthetic. Concrete. Mat tape. Chalk diagrams. The weight room. Sweat and grind and repetition.

No celebration. Just control.

### Progression Tiers

| Tier | Identity | What It Means |
|------|----------|---------------|
| FOUNDATION | You know where you are | Stance, movement, basic position |
| CHAIN WRESTLING | You know what comes next | One attack flows into another |
| PRESSURE | You take what you want | Sustained top control, riding, breakdowns |
| SCRAMBLE | You win the chaos | Recovery, reversal, read-and-react |
| COMPETITOR | You dictate the match | Pacing, strategy, composure under fatigue |
| ELITE | You're the problem | Coaches point at you in the room |

### Visual System: The Forged Chain

Each tier unlocks a chain link in the visual. The chain grows from Foundation to Elite. When a new link is forged, the animation is slow and heavy — not a celebration, a completion. The chain metaphor runs through every wrestling screen: technique trees use chain nodes, tier progress uses link forging, the 90-day map shows the chain being built week by week.

The chain is also functional: it shows the actual attack chains the athlete is learning. Single leg → finish vs. single leg → high-C → finish. The system makes those relationships visible.

### First 90 Days — Foundation Tier

The goal of the first 90 days is not to win. It is to learn how to apply pressure and understand that wrestling is a chain, not a collection of individual moves.

| Weeks | Focus | Drill | Small Win |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|
| 1–2 | Stance + Motion | Wrestling stance, level change, penetration step, movement patterns | Maintain stance for a full live round |
| 3–4 | Hand Fighting | Collar ties, inside control, pummeling, wrist ties | Win inside position consistently against a training partner |
| 5–6 | Single Leg — Entry + Finish | High-C setup, elevation finish, trip finish, posture on the shot | Finish one live takedown |
| 7–8 | Sprawl + Defensive Wrestling | Hip pressure, whizzer, go-behind, sprawl-to-mat return | Stuff a live shot cleanly |
| 9–10 | Top Pressure | Breakdowns (wrist/ankle), riding, hip control, chest pressure | Control a training partner for a full live round on top |
| 11–12 | Chain Wrestling | Single → double, reshot concepts, scramble entries, mat returns | Link two attacks in one sequence during live wrestling |
| 13 | Tier review | Coach evaluation of chain wrestling fundamentals | Be able to name the chain coming off your best shot |

### What Earns Foundation → Chain Wrestling Tier

- ☐ Maintains wrestling stance under conditioning fatigue
- ☐ Demonstrates safe and sound shot mechanics (no knee-drop)
- ☐ Understands at least one full attack chain (entry → two finish options)
- ☐ Shows positional pressure on top — not just takedown, control
- ☐ Wins inside position in pummeling against a peer
- ☐ Meets conditioning expectation (set by coach per gym)
- ☐ Demonstrates discipline and consistency: 24+ sessions logged
- ☐ Coach signoff — positional and attitude evaluation

### What Earns Pressure Tier (Tier 3)

- ☐ 50+ live go rounds logged
- ☐ Master 6 documented takedown chains
- ☐ Maintain 70%+ mat return rate in live wrestling
- ☐ Win 5 situational sparring rounds (coach-set scenarios)
- ☐ Complete 3 conditioning pace tests at tier standard
- ☐ Coach evaluation: pressure, tenacity, composure
- ☐ Final live assessment

### Copy Voice for Wrestling

Wrestling copy is the driest in the app. No emotional language. Numbers and facts.

**Good:**
- "64% takedown rate. Up 8% this month."
- "6 of 12 chains mastered."
- "Mat return rate: 72%. Tier standard: 70%."

**Bad:**
- "Great work this week!"
- "You're crushing it!"
- "Keep pushing!"

The athlete's result is the encouragement. The system doesn't add to it.

### App Experience Notes

The wrestling room screen should feel like you're looking at a whiteboard in a wrestling room — chalk diagrams, chain trees, tactical systems. Not a dashboard. A wall.

Frankie Edgar will look at this and immediately recognize it as wrestling culture. The chain system is novel enough to be interesting. The language is authentic enough to be respected. The progression is earned enough to mean something.

# DISCIPLINE 3: MUAY THAI
## "The art of eight limbs. Earned through repetition, composure, and heart."

### Cultural Identity

Muay Thai is a ritual sport. Before you're a fighter, you're a student of weapons. The progression isn't about winning — it's about developing each weapon until it belongs to you. Timing. Repetition. Composure. Rhythm.

The culture is not Western fitness culture. It's ancient and intentional. You earn each weapon by mastering it under pressure, not by completing a checklist. The coach's eye is the final authority — always.

The visual language: prajioud armbands (the traditional Muay Thai armband worn above the bicep). Each level is marked by an armband that gains color, weave, and weight. Wrapped rope. Leather pads. Oxidized bronze. Crimson. Sweat-stained cotton. The ring at night with a single overhead light.

Not wellness. Not yoga. Not sport karate. Muay Thai.

### Progression Tiers — The Prajioud System

| Tier | Name | Weapons Focus |
|------|------|---------------|
| 1 | STANCE & COMPOSURE | Footwork, guard, teep, straight punches |
| 2 | THE WEAPONS | Roundhouse, defense, checks |
| 3 | THE CLINCH | Clinch posture, knees, sweeps |
| 4 | COMBINATIONS | Offense in chains, ring IQ, timing |
| 5 | FIGHT MIND | Composure in sparring, pacing, pressure |

The prajioud visual: a wrapped armband that begins bare cotton and gains color markings at each tier. At Tier 5, it's fully marked. The app renders a photorealistic armband that updates when a new tier is reached.

"Each mark is a weapon earned. Not given."

### First 90 Days — Tier 1: Stance & Composure

The goal: survive class confidently and move without fear. This tier is not about power. It is about foundation.

| Weeks | Focus | Drill | Small Win |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|
| 1–2 | Stance + Footwork | Muay Thai stance, guard position, forward/lateral movement, teep mechanics | Move a full round without crossing your feet |
| 3–4 | Jab + Cross | Straight punches, breathing on contact, return to guard | Land jab-cross on pads cleanly with snap and return |
| 5–6 | Checks + Defense | Leg check mechanics, shell guard, distance management | Successfully check kicks during a drilling round |
| 7–8 | Round Kick | Hip rotation, shin contact point, balance recovery after kick | Throw 20 clean kicks per side with coach observation |
| 9–10 | Clinch Introduction | Clinch posture, hand fighting, basic knee mechanics | Maintain posture for a full clinch round without breaking guard |
| 11–12 | Combinations | Jab → kick, teep → cross, defense into offense | Chain 3 weapons together on pads |
| 13 | Tier review | Coach evaluation | Move with composure. Throw with intention. Defend with awareness. |

### What Earns Tier 1 → Tier 2

- ☐ Maintains Muay Thai stance under pressure for full rounds
- ☐ Throws technically sound jab, cross, teep, and round kick
- ☐ Demonstrates defensive awareness: check, shell, distance management
- ☐ Shows composure during live drills — not frozen, not panicked
- ☐ 24+ sessions logged
- ☐ Demonstrates respect and consistency — Iron Army standard
- ☐ Coach signoff

### Copy Voice for Muay Thai

More atmosphere than wrestling. Still dry — but the language should feel like a fighter's internal monologue, not a coach's evaluation.

**Good:**
- "1,247 teeps landed. 326 this month."
- "Clinch sessions: 12. Composure held: 10 of 12."
- "Tier 2 requires the clinch. You're building toward it."

**Bad:**
- Any wellness language
- Any meditation language
- "You're doing amazing"

### App Experience Notes

The Muay Thai home screen should feel like you're looking at a gym in Bangkok before a fight night — dark, warm, focused. The armband visual is the emotional anchor of this discipline. The coach-verified badge on Muay Thai should feel earned and weighty, not like a certification sticker.

# DISCIPLINE 4: BOXING
## "Sweet science. Sharp mind. Strong heart."

### Cultural Identity

Boxing is not about power. Beginners think it's about punching hard. The sport is about timing, distance, and decision-making. The best boxers in the world talk about the sport the way chess players talk about chess — with precision, patience, and deep respect for technique over aggression.

The progression system must reflect this. You don't advance by how hard you hit. You advance by how smart and composed you are.

The visual language: handwraps. Leather. Corner stools. Ring ropes under amber light. Fight posters on the wall. Worn speed bags. The smell of leather and sweat. The sound of a timer.

Clean but not sterile. Precise but not corporate.

### Progression Tiers — Technician System

| Tier | Name | Identity |
|------|------|----------|
| FOUNDATION | Building the base | Stance, jab, footwork, defense |
| TECHNICIAN LEVEL 1 | Fundamentals mastered | Range, guard, straight punches, basic combos |
| TECHNICIAN LEVEL 2 | Defense installed | Head movement, slipping, blocking, counter-jab |
| TECHNICIAN LEVEL 3 | Combinations flowing | 3–5 punch combinations, counter timing, ring control |
| FIGHTER LEVEL 4 | Live environment | Sparring composure, pacing, pressure, strategy |
| ELITE LEVEL 5 | The Sweet Science | Full integration — offense, defense, IQ, fight management |

The visual: wrapped hands that change as tiers progress. At Foundation, bare knuckle wrap. By Level 5, fully wrapped fighter's hands. The wrapping process itself as the visual metaphor — methodical, intentional, never rushed.

### First 90 Days — Foundation Tier

The goal: understand that boxing is a system, not a collection of punches. Build the posture and habits that support everything else.

| Weeks | Focus | Drill | Small Win |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|
| 1–2 | Stance + Guard | Orthodox/southpaw stance, guard position, stance movement | Hold proper guard for an entire pad round |
| 3–4 | The Jab | Jab mechanics, extension, shoulder protection, return path | Land 50 clean jabs on the bag in a single round |
| 5–6 | Jab + Cross | 1-2, weight transfer, breathing on contact | Land 1-2 on pads with snap and guard return |
| 7–8 | Footwork + Range | In/out movement, pivot, angle creation, range reading | Create one clean angle per round in partner work |
| 9–10 | Defense Introduction | Slip, block, shell, pull-back | Slip a jab in a drilling round without flinching |
| 11–12 | 3-punch combinations | 1-2-3, 1-2-hook, basic counter combos | Complete a 3-punch combination on pads with rhythm |
| 13 | Tier review | Coach pad session + evaluation | Demonstrate stance, jab, one combination, one slip |

### What Earns Foundation → Technician Level 1

- ☐ Maintains proper boxing stance and guard under fatigue
- ☐ Jab accuracy at 65%+ in pad sessions
- ☐ Demonstrates 1-2 combination with power, timing, and return
- ☐ Shows basic footwork patterns without breaking stance
- ☐ Demonstrates one defensive technique in a controlled drill
- ☐ 24+ sessions logged
- ☐ Coach signoff

### What Earns Technician Level 3 → Fighter Level 4

- ☐ 36 boxing classes attended
- ☐ 30 technical drills completed
- ☐ Jab accuracy 70%+ in live rounds
- ☐ 20 spar rounds (3+ rounds per session)
- ☐ Demonstrate 3–5 punch combinations in 5 of 5 coach observations
- ☐ Defense: slip, weave, and block proficiency (4 of 5)
- ☐ Counter successfully 10+ times in sparring
- ☐ Conditioning test at Level 3 standard
- ☐ Coach evaluation and final approval

### Copy Voice for Boxing

Precise. Controlled. The language of craft.

**Good:**
- "72% jab accuracy. 5% above tier standard."
- "Defense success: 61%. Ring control time: 1:38 avg."
- "6 of 9 requirements completed."

**Bad:**
- Power language ("crushing," "destroying")
- Motivational filler
- Vague feedback ("getting better every day")

### App Experience Notes

Boxing screens should feel like looking at fight footage on an old monitor in the corner of a gym. The skill breakdown should look like a technical scouting report, not a fitness tracker. Every metric is a craft metric — not a fitness metric.

# DISCIPLINE 5: MMA
## "Integrate. Adapt. Dominate."

### Cultural Identity

MMA is not a discipline. It is the test of all disciplines.

The MMA progression system is fundamentally different from the others because there is no "MMA technique" to drill in isolation. The skill is integration — and the progression reflects that.

You don't advance by learning more techniques. You advance by proving you can apply what you know across contexts, under pressure, against resistance. The milestones are not technique-based. They are state-based.

The visual language: octagon geometry. Tactical dossiers. Fight camp dry-erase boards. Mission briefings. Dark steel under amber light. Game plan on the wall. "PRESSURE. CONTROL. FINISH."

The athlete should feel: "I am entering camp." Not: "I am completing a training module."

### Progression Tiers — Readiness System

| Stage | Name | Identity |
|-------|------|----------|
| 1 | FOUNDATION | Building the raw materials |
| 2 | AMATEUR READY | Can compete without embarrassing yourself |
| 3 | CAMP READY | System integration beginning to hold under pressure |
| 4 | COMPETITION READY | Consistent, dangerous, coachable |
| 5 | PRO READY | Fight IQ, pacing, composure — all present |
| 6 | ELITE | You are the problem |

The visual: the Skill Integration Map — a hexagonal system showing the six connections between disciplines (striking-to-takedown, takedown-to-top-control, top-control-to-submissions, submissions-to-scrambles, scrambles-to-feet, feet-to-striking). As the athlete progresses, the connections light up. Foundation has no lit connections. Elite has all six.

This is the central visual concept of the MMA progression screens. The hexagon is the brand mark of MMA in Cage Logic.

### Five Pillars of MMA Development

Each pillar is tracked separately and combines into the tier score.

| Pillar | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| STRIKING | Boxing, Muay Thai application in MMA context |
| GRAPPLING | BJJ and submission application under MMA rules |
| WRESTLING | Takedowns, mat returns, cage control |
| CONDITIONING | Pace, round management, recovery under fatigue |
| FIGHT IQ | Decision-making, transitions, composure, adaptation |

### First 90 Days — Foundation Tier

The goal: learn how to move between worlds without thinking. The first 90 days are about removing the gaps — the moment you freeze between striking and grappling, between takedown and top control.

| Weeks | Focus | Drill | Small Win |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|
| 1–2 | Stance Switching | Orthodox/southpaw transition, range management across zones, reactive stance | Move smoothly from striking to clinch range in one motion |
| 3–4 | Strike → Shot Connections | Jab → single leg, cross → double, teep → level change | Complete a strike-to-takedown in a drilling round |
| 5–6 | Wall Work | Cage entries, cage escapes, dirty boxing, cage takedowns | Control a training partner on the wall for 30 seconds |
| 7–8 | Ground Striking | Posture in top control, ground-and-pound mechanics, damage vs. position balance | Land 5 clean strikes in top control during a live round |
| 9–10 | Scramble Control | Recovery off the bottom, re-shoot off the scramble, turtle position | Win one scramble to top position in live rolling |
| 11–12 | Round Management | Pace variation, takedown timing, clock awareness, when to stand vs. stay | Manage a 5-minute round with obvious tactical intention |
| 13 | Foundation review | Live MMA flow evaluation | Coach can identify a game plan in your rolling — even a basic one |

### What Earns Foundation → Amateur Ready

- ☐ 36 MMA classes attended
- ☐ 20 live sparring rounds (MMA rules, light contact)
- ☐ Demonstrate all takedown entries (12 documented)
- ☐ Submit 3 different submission types from different positions
- ☐ Complete Strength & Power program at Foundation level (4 of 6 units)
- ☐ Fight IQ assessment — coach evaluated
- ☐ Official sparring assessment — coach-observed live round
- ☐ Coach final evaluation

### The Fight Readiness Tracker

MMA is the only discipline with a cross-pillar readiness tracker. Four quadrants tracked daily:

| Metric | Source |
|--------|--------|
| STRENGTH | Training load data |
| ENDURANCE | Session duration + round completions |
| RECOVERY | Soreness + rest day tracking |
| MENTAL FOCUS | Psych check-in data (from mental module) |

These four scores combine into a single **Camp Readiness** number. That number drives the athlete's daily recommendation. If recovery is low and a hard session is logged, the system flags it. Not with a warning. With a fact: "Recovery: 6.8/10. High-intensity camp session scheduled."

### Copy Voice for MMA

Tactical. Concise. Mission-level.

**Good:**
- "Takedown success: 57%. Integration target: 65%."
- "Camp Prep: 3 weeks out."
- "Scramble sessions: 8. Win rate: 63%."
- Session ratings: GOOD / SOLID / GREAT — not scores, not stars

**Bad:**
- Any wellness or recovery language that sounds medical
- Overexplained metrics
- "You're making great progress"

### App Experience Notes

The MMA home screen is the most information-dense in the app. It should feel like a tactical briefing room — organized, intentional, no wasted space. The Skill Integration Map is the hero visual. Everything else supports it.

When a connection lights up on the hexagon for the first time, that is a significant moment. The animation should be slow, amber, and permanent-feeling. Not a notification. A record.

## CROSS-DISCIPLINE SYSTEM RULES

### What Every Discipline Shares

1. **Weekly structure** — something specific to work on this week
2. **Small win** — the moment you feel it click
3. **Milestone checklist** — coach-validated, not self-reported
4. **Coach signoff** — the human authority that makes it mean something
5. **Identity progression** — who you are in this sport is changing

### What Each Discipline Protects

Each discipline must never be reduced to the others. Specifically:
- Wrestling never feels like "BJJ with takedowns"
- Boxing never feels like "Muay Thai without kicks"
- MMA never feels like "a combination of the others"

Each has its own screen vocabulary, copy voice, visual identity, and emotional register. Unified product. Different cultures.

### The Coach Verification System

Every tier advancement across all disciplines requires coach signoff. This is non-negotiable. The system can track reps, sessions, and metrics automatically — but the tier does not advance without human authority.

This is intentional. It mirrors how these sports actually work. The app does not promote athletes. Coaches do. The app documents the evidence that makes the decision easy.

Badge language: **COACH VERIFIED** — not "Approved" or "Certified" or "Validated." Coach verified.

### The 90-Day Design Principle

The first 90 days of every discipline are designed around one insight from the Iron Army BJJ doc:

> "Weeks 5–8: The wall. This is the dangerous stretch. You've been training long enough to feel bad about your progress but not long enough to feel good about it. Most people quit here."

Every discipline's 90-day map has a built-in inflection point at weeks 5–8 where the small wins are largest and most specific. This is by design. The system targets the exact moment athletes are most likely to quit and gives them the most visible reason to stay.

That is the product's core promise: **make invisible progress visible.**

## The problem you already know

Most BJJ gyms lose the majority of their white belts inside the first year. Industry 12-month retention sits around **35%** — most people who walk through the door are gone within a year, and they quit in a predictable window: **weeks 5–8**, the wall, when they've trained long enough to feel bad about their progress but not long enough to feel good about it.

## The leak

At ~35% retention you lose roughly **52 members a year.**

> 52 members × ~$3,150 LTV ≈ **$163,800 a year walking out the door.**

## The fix — what Cage Logic changes

A **+15% retention lift** is modest and realistic — it means catching a fraction of the people who quit at the wall.

> +15% ≈ **12 more members saved a year** × ~$3,500 LTV ≈ **$42,000/year in recovered revenue.**

## The ROI

Iron Army founder rate is **$49/month** for the gym (≈$588/year); your members ride permanently locked member rates.

> ~$42,000 recovered against a few hundred a year in cost = **15–30× ROI in year one.** Even at *half* the lift, it pays for itself many times over.

## How it actually retains — the mechanism, not magic

- **Captures each student's "why" on day one** — and surfaces it back at the exact moment they want to quit.
- **Names the weeks 5–8 wall before they hit it**, and runs a dropout-prevention prompt right when most people quit ("most people quit here — don't").
- **Shows you who's drifting** — at-risk flags (7-day soft / 14-day hard) so you reach out *before* they ghost, while it still matters.
- **The Investment Score rewards the thing that makes white belts into blue belts** — showing up, consistently.

## Bottom line

You asked for a way to keep students without building another app. This is the model. It's built on your onboarding, your curriculum, your philosophy — and the math says it pays for itself many times over the first year.

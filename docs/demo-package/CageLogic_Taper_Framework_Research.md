## What the experts agree on

**Sports science (Mujika & Padilla, the foundational tapering research):** a taper is a *progressive* (non-linear) reduction in training load. Cut **volume** substantially (commonly 40–60%), **maintain intensity**, reduce **frequency** only slightly (<20%), over ~1–2 weeks. Progressive tapers beat step-downs. Typical gain ~3%.

**BJJ/grappling specific (Breaking Muscle, Grapplearts/Kesting, JiuJitsu.com):** intensity peaks ~2 weeks out (~RPE 8), then drops below RPE 6 in the final week. Final week is sharpness + recovery, not fitness-building. No new techniques. A few high-intensity but low-volume rounds early in comp week (safety over intensity, stop early), hard sparring tapers off 2–3 days out, full rest the day before, prioritize sleep and nutrition.

## Recommended framework (Saturday competition)

| Time out | Volume | Intensity / sparring | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4+ weeks | Baseline (normal) | Full, building | Hard block — this is where the work is banked |
| ~2 weeks out | Baseline, begin slight trim | Intensity peaks (~RPE 8), full sparring | Last hard block. **No new techniques from here** — trust your A-game |
| Comp week, Mon–Wed | Reduced ~40–50% | High-intensity, **low-volume**, shorter sessions; safety > intensity | A few sharp technical/positional rounds; stop early |
| Comp week, Thu–Fri | Light | Technical/positional only; drop below ~RPE 6 | Hard sparring off 2–3 days out |
| Day before | Rest or ~15 min light drill | None | Prioritize sleep + nutrition |
| Competition day | — | Compete | — |

## App implementation notes

- Present as a **framework, not a mandate** — coach/athlete adjust. Research-backed language, no motivational tone (brand voice).
- The personalization question already specced ("How do you feel the week of competition? — more rest / normal until 2–3 days out / active right up to comp") shifts the final-week aggressiveness.
- This is the **default**; the coach can override.

# PART 1 — BJJ
## "Your journey. Earned. Not given."

The belt is not an award. It is a document of what happened on the mat. The stripe system at Iron Army follows community standards built over decades of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu lineage. No stripe is given — each one is observed, evaluated, and verified by a coach who has stood where you are.

Minimum age for blue belt: 16. Minimum age for black belt: 19.
Time minimums are floors, not targets. Many take longer. That is not failure.

---

### WHITE BELT — NO STRIPE
**Identity:** You just walked in. You don't know what you don't know yet.

**What this phase is:**
The first few weeks are disorienting on purpose. You will be submitted. You will be lost. You will not know where you are in the roll. That is the point. Orientation is the skill.

**Technical focus:**
- Posture and base: what it means to be "in base" on the mat
- Framing: the two primary frames (shoulder/hip) that prevent smashing in side control
- The shrimp: hip escape mechanics; the foundation of all bottom movement
- Technical stand-up: stand safely from turtle without exposing the back
- Tapping: correct tap mechanics, when and why — ego does not survive this sport

**Session requirement:** 8+ classes
**Coach criteria:** attended orientation, understands tap mechanics, asks questions in class

---

### WHITE BELT — STRIPE 1
**Identity:** You can survive. You're not going to get hurt by staying calm.

**Technical requirements:**
- Shrimp escape (elbow-knee): from side control, recover to guard or knees
- Frame in side control: far shoulder and hip frame; hold for a full drilling round
- Closed guard: pull to closed guard from bottom, break posture (two-on-one wrist control)
- Guard retention: knee shield (half butterfly), hip escape to re-guard
- Rear naked choke: proper lock mechanics — palm-to-palm vs. hand-to-bicep, chin tuck
- Turtle position: proper posture, prevent opponent from taking your back

**Performance standards:**
- Escape side control to guard or knees at least once in a live round (same-size partner)
- Hold closed guard for a full round without giving it up
- Survive 3 live rounds without injury (tapping freely = correct, not failure)

**Session requirement:** 24+ total classes (Iron Army standard: 2x/week minimum × 12 weeks)
**Conditioning:** complete class warm-up without stopping
**Coach criteria:** demonstrates shrimp and frame under light resistance; taps correctly; shows up

---

### WHITE BELT — STRIPE 2
**Identity:** You have a bottom game. You've made someone work to submit you.

**Technical requirements:**
- Hip bump sweep (closed guard): setup, weight shift, finish to mount
- Scissor sweep (closed guard): knee positioning, pull-and-kick mechanics, finish
- Armbar from closed guard: open-to-armbar entry (hip out, leg over face, finish)
- Triangle choke: leg positioning, pull-to-triangle, head control (finish not required — position is)
- Mount escape — bridge and roll (upa): timing against high mount
- Mount escape — elbow-knee (technical): create space, recover to half guard or guard
- Back control: hooks in, seatbelt grip, chin strap awareness — hold for 20 seconds

**Performance standards:**
- Land a sweep in live rolling (any sweep, any opponent)
- Escape mount twice in a single session
- Attempt (not necessarily finish) one submission in live rolling

**Session requirement:** 48+ total
**Conditioning:** survive 4 live rounds (5 minutes each) in a single training session
**Coach criteria:** demonstrates sweep mechanics; attempts submissions in rolling (not just defending)

---

### WHITE BELT — STRIPE 3
**Identity:** You have a top game. You're starting to make the mat work for you.

**Technical requirements:**
- Knee cut pass: from half guard and from open guard; hips through, shoulder pressure, finish to side control
- Torreando pass (basic): grip both pants at the knee, redirect to either side, establish control
- Side control: top maintenance — hip-to-hip connection, far-side underhook, weight distribution
- Mount: high mount (move from basic to high mount), arm triangle entry setup
- Back control full sequence: take the back from turtle, insert hooks, maintain with seatbelt
- Single leg takedown: level change, penetration step, drive-through finish OR trip — live attempt required
- Pummeling: inside position awareness, underhook fighting for 2-minute drilling round

**Performance standards:**
- Pass a training partner's guard in live rolling (at least once in a session)
- Maintain mount for 20+ seconds against active resistance
- Attempt a takedown in live rolling

**Session requirement:** 72+ total
**Conditioning:** complete two full rounds with a rolling partner at a pace that challenges both
**Coach criteria:** can explain why they're passing (not just doing it); takedown attempt shows correct mechanics

---

### WHITE BELT — STRIPE 4
**Identity:** Competition or promotion-ready. A personal game is emerging.

**Technical requirements:**
- One functional guard: closed, half, or butterfly — athlete selects, coach confirms it works
- One reliable pass: must work consistently in drilling vs. same level
- Two submission threats from different positions (e.g., armbar from guard + RNC from back)
- Leg lock awareness: knows what a heel hook is, knows the danger, knows the defense posture (not executing)
- Takedown with finish: single leg or double leg with a clean live finish (not just level change)
- Chain two offensive positions in one live roll: takedown → pass → mount, or sweep → mount → back take

**Performance standards:**
- Submit a training partner in live rolling (at least once — coach-appropriate pairing)
- Coach can describe athlete's game in two sentences

**Session requirement:** 96+ total (approximately 1 year of 2x/week training)
**Competition or equivalent:** attended 1 competition OR 8+ hard rolling sessions with upper belts
**Character standard:** actively helps newer white belts; asks questions; shows accountability on the mat
**Coach criteria:** initiate blue belt evaluation conversation; athlete ready to be known as a blue belt

---

## BLUE BELT
**Awarded.** The first real identity shift. Not just surviving — beginning to develop.
*Minimum age 16. Minimum time at white belt: coach discretion, typically 2 years. Coach recommendation required.*

---

### BLUE BELT — STRIPE 1
**Identity:** You're finding your game. Not everything works yet, but something does.

**Technical requirements:**
- Primary guard: one working guard with 3+ sweep options and 2+ submission entries
- Guard pass: one reliable pass with a backup when the first is defended
- Submission from closed guard: armbar or triangle to completion in drilling (not just entry)
- Submission from top: Americana, kimura, or north-south choke — one that works in live rolling
- Leg lock awareness upgraded: can identify ashi garami being entered and escape to safe position
- Takedown: one entry, two finish options (e.g., single leg: drive-through finish + trip finish)

**Performance standards:**
- Submits blue belts in rolling on a consistent basis (not every roll — reliably)
- Competitive in rolling with purple belts (survives, creates trouble, not dominated)

**Session requirement:** 120+ from blue belt promotion
**Coach criteria:** personal game is identifiable; athlete stops relying on strength as primary tool

---

### BLUE BELT — STRIPE 2
**Identity:** Your back attack is dangerous. You understand the submission game.

**Technical requirements:**
- Back take: minimum 3 entries — from turtle, from guard pass, from scramble
- Rear naked choke: finish from back control on active, resistant partner
- Triangle choke: full sequence — entry, leg positioning, adjustment, squeeze, finish
- Armbar: mount-to-armbar and guard-to-armbar; can transition when one is defended
- Guillotine: standing arm-in, guard-pull guillotine — one working version
- Half guard: one functional half guard with at least 1 sweep and guard recovery option

**Performance standards:**
- Submit training partners from back position in live rolling
- Maintain top control for full rounds against peer-level opponents

**Session requirement:** 160+ from blue belt promotion
**Coach criteria:** submission attempts are now submissions — finishing, not just threatening

---

### BLUE BELT — STRIPE 3
**Identity:** Your passing game is developing. You're becoming hard to hold.

**Technical requirements:**
- Torreando pass: two directional variations, can switch when one is defended
- Leg drag concept: hip position, knee cut to leg drag connection
- Double underhook pass: posture, drive, finish to side control or mount
- Mount: technical mount to S-mount; arm triangle from mount setup and finish
- Leg lock awareness (advanced): heel hook entry positions identified; defense to single-leg X from all ashi variations
- Closed guard: full combination sequences — sweep/armbar/triangle linked (attempt one, threaten all three)

**Performance standards:**
- Competition attendance: 1+ events (gi or no-gi) OR equivalent: 15+ hard sparring rounds against upper belts
- Can be described by coach as having a defined game

**Session requirement:** 200+ from blue belt promotion
**Conditioning:** complete a full competitive-length match (5 minutes) at full effort with purpose
**Coach criteria:** now a resource for white belts — trusted to answer technical questions on the mat

---

### BLUE BELT — STRIPE 4
**Identity:** Near-purple proficiency. Teaching what you know. Personal game is real.

**Technical requirements:**
- Leg locks: straight ankle lock and kneebar entry (outside heels only) executed safely in controlled rounds
- Two functional guards: primary fully developed, secondary being built
- Passing: three distinct styles — pressure, leg drag/torreando, and one toreando counter when guard is deep
- Takedowns: two working entries, one reliable live finish
- Chain three: link three offensive positions in one roll (example: double leg → knee cut → mount → arm triangle)
- Teaching: demonstrate and accurately explain any white belt technique when asked

**Performance standards:**
- Can describe their own A-game in detail — what they want, how they get there, what they do when it's defended
- Actively coaches white belts in supervised drilling without being asked

**Session requirement:** 240+ from blue belt promotion
**Competition or equivalent:** 3 events OR 25+ hard rounds against purple/brown belts
**Character standard:** known in the room as someone who makes training partners better
**Coach criteria:** purple belt evaluation initiated

---

## PURPLE BELT
**Awarded.** The knowledge belt. The serious practitioner. The hard years.
*Minimum age 16. Minimum 2 years at blue belt. Coach recommendation required.*

---

### PURPLE BELT — STRIPE 1
**Identity:** Your game has structure. You know what you're building.

**Technical requirements:**
- Primary guard fully operational: 3 sweeps, 2 submissions, active pass prevention
- Counter wrestling: sprawl with hip pressure, front headlock series (guillotine entry, darce entry), re-shot off sprawl
- No-gi application: core gi game transfers without grips — can roll no-gi at blue belt level minimum
- Heel hooks (outside): controlled entry and finish in drilling with advanced training partners
- X-guard entry: from single leg, from standing opponent, basic sweep

**Performance standards:**
- Dominant majority of rolls against blue belts
- Competitive with brown belts — creating genuine problems, not just surviving

**Session requirement:** 100+ from purple belt promotion
**Coach criteria:** visible growth beyond blue belt — something new is working that wasn't there before

---

### PURPLE BELT — STRIPE 2
**Identity:** Your back system is developed. Leg locks are in the game.

**Technical requirements:**
- Inside heel hooks: awareness, entry identification, and defense protocol; limited live execution (rolling partner skill level dependent — coach judgment)
- Ashi garami: basic outside, inside, and cross ashi positions; break attempts and guard recovery from all three
- Back system: 3 entries → RNC → if defended → bow and arrow → if defended → cross collar or armbar — full sequence in drilling
- Guard recovery under passing pressure: hip escape timing, knee shield replacement, inversion entries
- Berimbolo concept: can enter and understand the positional logic (finish not required at this level)

**Performance standards:**
- Competitive with brown belts in majority of rounds
- Leg lock entries are intentional, not accidental

**Session requirement:** 150+ from purple belt promotion
**Competition or equivalent:** 5 events or 30+ hard rounds against brown and black belts
**Coach criteria:** student can now help teach leg lock safety and mechanics to lower belts

---

### PURPLE BELT — STRIPE 3
**Identity:** Competition proficiency. A second guard is operational. You're dangerous in multiple positions.

**Technical requirements:**
- Second guard developed: if primary is closed → now half, butterfly, or leg drag; if primary is open → now closed or rubber guard concept
- Inside heel hook: controlled execution in advanced training partner context (coach-approved pairing required)
- Pressure passing AND movement passing: can identify which system suits the opponent and switch
- Competition history: 3+ events with results, OR coach discretion equivalent for non-competitors
- Teaching: can run a class and accurately teach blue belt curriculum

**Performance standards:**
- Taps brown belts on a consistent enough basis to be considered a threat — not consistently, but regularly
- Described by training partners at blue belt as someone they specifically do not want to roll with

**Session requirement:** 200+ from purple belt promotion
**Coach criteria:** approaching brown belt conversation — the question is when, not if

---

### PURPLE BELT — STRIPE 4
**Identity:** Near-brown proficiency. Defined game. Known in the room.

**Technical requirements:**
- Defined A-game: coach describes it in two sentences without hesitation
- Leg lock system integrated: leg locks connect naturally to passing game and guard game — not isolated attacks
- Full guard repertoire: can play functionally from any common guard position when forced there
- Takedowns: 2+ working entries with reliable live finishes
- Character: a leader on the mat — newer practitioners are coached, not dismissed

**Performance standards:**
- Brown belts and black belts take this athlete seriously in rolling

**Session requirement:** 250+ from purple belt promotion
**Competition or equivalent:** 5+ events, OR coach + brown belt consensus that competition readiness is present
**Coach criteria:** brown belt evaluation formally initiated

---

## BROWN BELT
**Awarded.** Near mastery. The refinement belt.
*Minimum age 18. Minimum 18 months at purple belt. Coach recommendation required.*

---

### BROWN BELT — STRIPE 1
**Identity:** You're refining what works. You're filling in the holes.

**Technical requirements:**
- Full leg lock system: entry → finish chain connected to passing system and guard game
- Turtle attacks: clock choke, leg drag from turtle, shoulder roll, Turk (leg drag to back take)
- Defensive wrestling complete: no-panic response available from all common positions — no position causes freeze
- Teaching: can run a structured class on any topic, including purple belt topics
- Ankle picks and foot sweeps from standup: minimum two working entries

**Performance standards:**
- Competitive with black belts — not winning most rounds, but landing submissions and creating real problems
- Black belts describe this athlete as "dangerous"

**Session requirement:** 120+ from brown belt promotion
**Coach criteria:** coaching ability is as important as rolling ability at this stage

---

### BROWN BELT — STRIPE 2
**Identity:** Your defensive game matches your offense. You control the pace.

**Technical requirements:**
- Scramble control: can choose to scramble (chaotic) or slow down (methodical) — both options available by choice
- Submission defense: identifies the attack before it's fully locked — defense is anticipatory, not reactive
- Fatigue game: can slow a rolling partner down by controlling pace, grips, and position
- Judo entries: osoto gari, kouchi gari, ouchi gari — at least two viable live entries against resisting partners
- Systematic study: athlete has identified one structural weakness and is actively addressing it (documented, coachable)

**Performance standards:**
- Recognized across the mat room as elite
- Black belts reference rolling with this athlete as a legitimate challenge

**Session requirement:** 160+ from brown belt promotion
**Coach criteria:** athlete knows what they know and what they don't know — rare self-awareness

---

### BROWN BELT — STRIPE 3
**Identity:** No major holes. The black belt is visible.

**Technical requirements:**
- All major submission families represented: joint locks (arm, shoulder, wrist), chokes (blood, air, neck crank), leg locks (ankle, knee, hip) — not all executed, but all present in the game
- Guard sweep against black belt: landed a sweep against a black belt training partner in live rolling (witnessed by coach)
- Instruction at all levels: can teach from white belt through purple belt effectively, including leg locks safely
- Competition record: consistent podium results at regional level, OR coach-certified equivalent depth of rolling

**Performance standards:**
- Black belt is a matter of time, not a question in the room

**Session requirement:** 200+ from brown belt promotion
**Character standard:** the first person newer students go to for help — not because it's assigned, because it's earned
**Coach criteria:** the black belt conversation is no longer hypothetical

---

### BROWN BELT — STRIPE 4
**Identity:** Black belt readiness. The room already knows.

**Requirements:**
- Age 19+ (IBJJF standard)
- Minimum 18 months at brown belt
- Coach initiation of black belt evaluation
- No technical holes the coach considers disqualifying
- Character: embodies the gym's culture — teaches without being asked, lifts everyone, competes with integrity
- The room already refers to this athlete as a black belt in conversation

**Coach criteria:** this is not a technique decision. It is a character, culture, and craft decision. The techniques are already there.

# PART 2 — WRESTLING
## "Forged. Not awarded."

Wrestling has no ceremony. No belt handed across a mat. Your rank is what you can do under fatigue, against resistance, when your legs are burning and the match is tied. The Cage Logic Chain System maps the real wrestling development journey — from surviving your first stance to dictating where every fight in the room goes.

The Chain System is built around one truth: **wrestling is not a collection of techniques. It is a sequence of reactions.** One attack creates the opening for the next. The system teaches that sequence explicitly — every tier adds links to your chain.

Visual: forged chain links. Each tier locks a new link. The chain doesn't get fancier. It gets heavier.

---

### TIER 1 — FOUNDATION
**Identity:** "You know where you are."
**What this means:** You understand wrestling stance, basic position, and the difference between safe and exposed. You're not winning yet, but you know what winning looks like.

**Chain 1 — Stance System**
Techniques:
- Wrestling stance: weight distribution, head position, hand position, feet parallel to shoulder width
- Level change: two methods (penetration step and drop step) — demonstrate on command without telegraphing
- Penetration step: knee to the mat (safe landing), drive through, posture recovery
- Movement: forward/backward/lateral in stance without crossing feet
- Hand position: lazy hands vs. active hands — can identify and correct in drilling

**Chain 2 — Hand Fighting**
Techniques:
- Collar tie: establish and control vs. live partner attempting to break
- Inside control: clear an overhook and establish underhook
- Pummeling: continuous inside-out fighting, winning inside 3 out of 5 exchanges
- Wrist tie: two-on-one setup from collar tie
- Arm drag: entry from inside position to back exposure

**Chain 3 — Single Leg Foundation**
Techniques:
- Single leg entry: from collar tie (inside step) and from level change (direct)
- Single leg position: head outside, knee on inside of opponent's thigh, elevation
- Single leg finish (1): elevation + trip (inside trip)
- Single leg finish (2): run-the-pipe (circle the leg)
- Sprawl defense: recognize the level change, hip pressure down, chest across the back

**Performance standards:**
- Maintain stance for a full live round without stance breaking
- Win inside position in pummeling 3 of 5 exchanges against a peer
- Attempt a live takedown with correct mechanics (finish not required)

**Session requirement:** 24+ wrestling-specific sessions
**Conditioning:** survive 3 live wrestling rounds (2 minutes each) without stopping
**Coach criteria:** stance holds under fatigue; no dangerous shot mechanics (knee safety)

---

### TIER 2 — CHAIN WRESTLING
**Identity:** "You know what comes next."
**What this means:** When your shot is defended, you don't panic and reset. You already know the next link. This tier is about connecting attacks, not perfecting individual moves.

**Chain 4 — Shot-to-Finish Chains**
Techniques:
- Single leg → high-C (when opponent posts and creates high leg): grip switch, finish to the mat
- Single leg → trip finish (when opponent sprawls and creates pressure): inside trip, outside trip
- Single leg → double leg (when opponent is slow to defend second leg): reach across, drive
- Double leg: level change, penetration, second leg capture, lift-and-drive or ankle-block finish
- Head-lock series: front headlock from failed shot (opponent's head drops), arm-in guillotine entry, shrug-by to back

**Chain 5 — Scramble and Counter Chains**
Techniques:
- Sprawl → mat return (when opponent's hands hit the mat): wrist control, hip control, gut wrench
- Sprawl → go-behind (when opponent turns to recover): step over, establish waist, control
- Go-behind → two-on-one: from behind position, hip to hip, control for back exposure
- Re-shot: when sprawled on, stand and immediately re-level change from same or opposite direction

**Chain 6 — Top Control Entry**
Techniques:
- Referee's position (top): standard position, legal hand/body placement
- Referee's position (bottom): base, chin up, hands inside
- Standup from bottom: base, create distance, referee allows — clean execution
- Tight waist: grip, drive opponent to belly, transition to front headlock

**Performance standards:**
- Link two attacks in one live sequence (e.g., single leg attempt → go to double leg on defense)
- Finish at least one live takedown per session (not just attempt)
- Maintain top control for a full 2-minute round without opponent escaping

**Session requirement:** 50+ wrestling sessions total
**Conditioning:** complete 4 live rounds with 30-second rest intervals
**Coach criteria:** athlete stops resetting to neutral after every failed attack; chains are beginning to appear naturally

---

### TIER 3 — PRESSURE
**Identity:** "You take what you want."
**What this means:** Top position is not just held — it is used. You can break an opponent down, apply near-fall pressure, and control the ride. You are the problem now.

**Chain 7 — Breakdown and Ride**
Techniques:
- Spiral ride: inside tricep and ankle, drive opponent to belly, maintain chest on back
- Tight waist breakdown: two-on-one grip break, drive opponent flat
- Cross-body ride: top position weight distribution, prevent standup for full round
- Wrist-and-ankle breakdown: wrist control, far ankle, tilt to back exposure
- Inside trip from top: from referee's top position, step inside, drive to back

**Chain 8 — Near Fall Series**
Techniques:
- Gut wrench: over/under grip from behind, barrel roll to near fall position
- Turk (leg-lace entry): from breakdown, leg lace mechanics, tilt to near fall
- Cradle (near cradle): head and knee, lock behind opponent's back, squeeze to nearfall
- Bar arm (arm bar to near fall): from top, far arm trapped, turn to near fall
- Half nelson to back: from belly-down, half nelson with tight waist, roll to back

**Chain 9 — Standup and Cage Control**
Techniques:
- Preventing standup: react to hip pressure, reset chest on back, stifle hand-to-mat
- Gut wrench from standing: opponent attempts standup, gut wrench counters before they're up
- Release and reattack: deliberately release, force re-engagement on your terms
- Setting up shots from hand fighting: collar tie feint to level change, inside trip from ties

**Performance standards:**
- Control top position for 2+ full 2-minute rounds (opponent cannot escape)
- Score near fall in at least 1 of 3 sessions (near fall criteria: 2 seconds back exposure)
- Takedown success rate above 50% in live wrestling (tracked by coach over 3 sessions)

**Session requirement:** 80+ wrestling sessions total
**Conditioning:** complete 5 rounds, 2 minutes each, at competitive pace — no stopping
**Coach criteria:** top pressure is intentional; breakdown positions set up follow-up attacks automatically

---

### TIER 4 — SCRAMBLE
**Identity:** "You win the chaos."
**What this means:** When both wrestlers hit the mat and neither has position, you know where you want to be and you get there. Scrambles terrify most wrestlers. This tier makes them your offense.

**Chain 10 — Scramble Reads**
Techniques:
- Scramble identification: recognize 4 primary scramble positions (both standing and exposed, both on mat, one up and one down, one behind)
- Scramble decision: for each position, identify the correct first move (not random reaction)
- Re-shoot off scramble: from scramble to immediate shot without resetting to stance
- Hip heist (Peterson roll): bottom position, opponent's overhook, hip heist mechanics to scramble to top
- Granby roll: defensive inversion to scramble recovery

**Chain 11 — Reversal System**
Techniques:
- Switch: from referee's bottom, execute switch to top before opponent's weight settles
- Stand-in-base: proper timing, hand clearing, clean standup from referee's bottom
- Rolls: forward roll from belly-down, opponent chasing, to top position
- Sit-out: sit out from referee's bottom, turn-in, recover to neutral
- Roll-through: opponent over-commits on breakdown, roll to top

**Chain 12 — Reattack After Stop**
Techniques:
- Reattack immediately: when a shot is stuffed, go to the second link in the chain before opponent resets
- Level change timing: use opponent's defensive motion as the setup — go when they push back
- Misdirection: feint high (head/collar), shoot low; feint level change, hand fight
- Tie-up to takedown: use tie-up to create a predictable reaction, attack off the reaction

**Performance standards:**
- Win scrambles: coach rates scramble win rate over 3 sessions (target: 60%+ scrambles won)
- Execute reversal once per session from referee's bottom position (against peer-level partner)
- Demonstrate misdirection setup leading to successful takedown entry

**Session requirement:** 110+ wrestling sessions total
**Conditioning:** 6 rounds, 2 minutes, 30-second rest — complete at tournament pace throughout
**Coach criteria:** athlete no longer freezes in scrambles; scramble positions are read, not reacted to blindly

---

### TIER 5 — COMPETITOR
**Identity:** "You dictate the match."
**What this means:** You choose where this match goes. Your shot selection is not based on habit — it's based on who you're facing, what round it is, and what score you need. This is wrestling intelligence.

**Chain 13 — Match Management**
Techniques:
- Riding time: understand the rule and use it strategically — ride when it costs nothing, release when it risks reversal
- Stalling recognition: can identify when opponent is stalling and use positioning and calls to counter it
- Clock awareness: knows exact score needed with 1 minute left, makes the right call
- Defending a lead: knows when to control and when to attack; doesn't give up a takedown late in a close match
- Attacking a deficit: knows how to force the action — doesn't wait for opponent to make a mistake

**Chain 14 — Opponent-Specific Selection**
Techniques:
- Scouting by observation: watch two rounds of an opponent, identify primary attacks and defensive tendencies
- Counter selection: identify which of your attacks beats their primary defense
- Tie-up preference adjustment: can switch dominant hand position based on opponent's reaction
- Alternate gameplan: if primary attack is taken away, what is the immediate adjustment?

**Chain 15 — Fatigue-Phase Wrestling**
Techniques:
- Second and third period wrestling: conditioning is not the same as fitness — can maintain technique execution when exhausted
- Grip strength under fatigue: maintains wrist and ankle control when arms are burning
- Stance under fatigue: catches stance breakdown in real time and self-corrects
- Attack under fatigue: executes takedown attempt in final 30 seconds of a hard round

**Performance standards:**
- Competition: 5+ dual meets or tournaments; consistent winning record (not required to be undefeated)
- Can articulate pre-match gameplan specific to each opponent before the match
- Visible tactical adjustment between periods (coach observes and confirms)

**Session requirement:** 150+ wrestling sessions total
**Conditioning:** complete 2 back-to-back 4-minute matches with 30-second rest — technique maintained throughout
**Coach criteria:** athlete can be trusted to execute a gameplan; coaching during a match changes outcomes

---

### TIER 6 — ELITE
**Identity:** "You are the problem in the room."
**What this means:** Coaches point at you when teaching. Training partners are better because they wrestled you. You have a signature system — a chain that is yours, that works, and that you can teach.

**Chain 16 — Signature System**
Requirements:
- Athlete can define their signature chain (3+ linked attacks) by name and demonstrate it in under 60 seconds
- Signature chain has been used successfully in competition or high-level sparring
- Alternative version exists for when the signature chain is scouted

**Chain 17 — Teaching All Chains**
Requirements:
- Can accurately teach all 15 chains from Tiers 1–5
- Can identify errors in a junior athlete's technique and correct them verbally and physically
- Has taught a Tier 1 or Tier 2 athlete who has advanced — teaching result visible

**Chain 18 — Adapt in Real Time**
Requirements:
- Can switch systems mid-round based on what the opponent is showing — observed and confirmed by coach
- Can explain after a live round what adjustment was made and why

**Session requirement:** 200+ wrestling sessions
**Competition:** consistent regional-level results; respected as a competitor outside this gym
**Coach criteria:** the coach learns something from rolling or watching this athlete — reciprocal teaching is present
**Note for Iron Army:** this tier is Frankie Edgar's standard. Not everyone reaches it. Most shouldn't expect to. That's what makes it mean something.

# PART 3 — MUAY THAI
## "The art of eight limbs. One weapon at a time."

Traditional Muay Thai was not built with belts. It was built with fights. The student fought, the student lost, the student came back, and eventually the student became dangerous. The Cage Logic Prajioud System adapts that philosophy for modern training: you don't earn the next level until the current weapon belongs to you — not when it's been drilled, but when it can be used under pressure.

The Prajioud (armband) is the visual anchor. In traditional Muay Thai culture, the prajioud is a sacred object — blessed before a fight, worn above the bicep, representing spiritual protection. The Cage Logic Prajioud visual starts as bare cotton and gains markings at each level. At Tier 5, it is fully marked. Every mark is a weapon earned.

**5 Tiers. 3 Levels each. 15 total levels.**

No armband is bought. No armband is given for attendance.

---

## TIER 1 — FOUNDATION
*"Build the structure everything else is built on."*
Armband: bare white cotton → first mark → second mark

---

### TIER 1 / LEVEL 1 — STANCE & GUARD
**Identity:** You can stand correctly and be hit without flinching.

**Technical requirements:**
- Muay Thai stance: feet shoulder-width, rear foot at 45°, weight balanced (60/40 front/rear), guard at chin, elbows close
- Guard: Thai guard position (hands high, elbows protecting ribs, chin down)
- Footwork: advance, retreat, lateral left, lateral right — no crossing feet for 2 minutes continuous
- Teep (push kick): lead leg teep — chamber, drive, extend, hip through, recover stance
- Breathing: exhale on contact; correct breathing maintained in pad round

**Performance standards:**
- Maintain correct stance for entire 3-round pad session
- Throw 30 clean teeps per side in a drilling round without balance loss
- Hold guard under light pressure (coach taps guard — does not collapse)

**Session requirement:** 16+ classes
**Conditioning:** complete full class warm-up including skip rope rounds
**Coach criteria:** stance is automatic; guard does not drop when tired

---

### TIER 1 / LEVEL 2 — THE STRAIGHT WEAPONS
**Identity:** Jab-cross is your handshake. Automatic. No thinking.

**Technical requirements:**
- Jab: relaxed shoulder, full extension, snap back — 50 clean jabs on the bag without stance collapse
- Cross: weight transfer, hip rotation, rear shoulder drives, fist lands at chin height, return to guard
- Jab-cross combination (1-2): rhythm, correct breathing, guard maintained before and after
- Footwork with punches: step with jab (advance), pivot off cross (45° angle creation)
- Return to guard: every combination ends in guard — no hands dropping after the last strike

**Performance standards:**
- Land 1-2 on pads with coach feedback: "snap and return" — coach confirms guard restored after combination
- Jab accuracy on focus mitts: 70%+ over a 3-minute round

**Session requirement:** 24+ classes
**Conditioning:** 3 rounds of 3 minutes on the heavy bag — stance and guard maintained throughout
**Coach criteria:** jab-cross is a habit, not a thought; no reset pause between jab and cross

---

### TIER 1 / LEVEL 3 — DEFENSE & DISTANCE
**Identity:** You know where you are in relation to your opponent. You can take a hit and keep your composure.

**Technical requirements:**
- Leg check (shin block): raise rear leg, turn knee out, intercept kick with shin, return to stance
- Shell defense: tuck chin, close elbows over ribs, absorb body shots without bending over
- Parry: lead hand redirects opponent's cross, rear hand guides away — not a flinch
- Distance management: use teep to create and maintain distance against advancing partner
- Clinch defense basics: hands inside, chin down, prevent head from being pulled down

**Performance standards:**
- Check 8 of 10 leg kicks in controlled drilling round (partner throws medium-speed kicks)
- Absorb 10 body shots in shell without posture collapse
- Teep advancing partner to maintain distance in 2-minute drilling round

**Session requirement:** 32+ classes
**Conditioning:** complete 4-round pad session without quality break
**Coach criteria:** defense is instinctive — athlete does not close eyes or flinch at the last moment

---

## TIER 2 — THE WEAPONS
*"Each weapon is earned before the next is taught."*
Armband: copper/brass cotton — two marks added over three levels

---

### TIER 2 / LEVEL 1 — THE ROUND KICK
**Identity:** The round kick belongs to you. Power, balance, recovery — all present.

**Technical requirements:**
- Round kick mechanics: pivot on support foot (90° rotation), hip drives through, shin as the weapon (not foot), knee height and above contact
- Lead leg round kick: setup with teep feint, kick to body, recover stance
- Rear leg round kick: switch step or direct chamber, full hip extension, full power version and speed version
- Round kick to the body: target — liver (lead kick), spleen (rear kick) — accuracy over power
- Round kick to the leg: low kick mechanics, inside leg kick vs. outside leg kick
- Balance recovery after kick: land in base, do not step forward unless in combination

**Performance standards:**
- Throw 20 round kicks per side at full effort — balance maintained on all 20
- Land rear leg round kick to body on pads in combination (1-2 → kick) with coach confirmation
- Low kick to leg: 10 clean contacts per side in drilling

**Session requirement:** 40+ classes
**Conditioning:** 3 rounds of 3 minutes — kicks thrown each round; no cramping or balance breakdown
**Coach criteria:** kick is a weapon, not a movement; coach would call it dangerous if thrown in sparring

---

### TIER 2 / LEVEL 2 — THE TEEP & THE KNEE
**Identity:** You control distance and the clinch. The teep is a weapon, not just a range tool.

**Technical requirements:**
- Teep as weapon: teep to the stomach with intent to push opponent off balance (not just extend)
- Teep to face: opponent bends over, lead teep to head — controlled in drilling
- Rear knee (basic): clinch engagement, knee to body, opponent's posture pulled into the knee
- Knees in the clinch: from double collar tie, pull opponent's head into knee — minimum 3 controlled reps
- Knee as counter: teep catches opponent mid-advance, drives them back
- Footwork under pressure: when backed against wall, create angle and re-center with teep

**Performance standards:**
- Teep: push training partner off balance 5 times in 2-minute round (partner attempts to advance)
- Knee to body: 10 clean knees in clinch drilling with correct posture
- Counter teep: time and land on advancing partner 3 times in drilling

**Session requirement:** 48+ classes
**Conditioning:** 4 rounds, 3 minutes each — include clinch rounds
**Coach criteria:** teep is not passive; knees are controlled and technically sound

---

### TIER 2 / LEVEL 3 — THE ELBOW
**Identity:** The elbow is Muay Thai's most dangerous weapon. It is earned last because it demands the most composure.

**Technical requirements:**
- Horizontal elbow: entry from clinch range, elbow travels parallel to floor, tip is the contact point — not forearm
- Diagonal elbow (downward): entry from above, slice downward — controlled in drilling only
- Elbow as counter: opponent throws and misses, follow-on elbow as counter on the same side
- Clinch + elbow: from double collar tie, create slight distance, horizontal elbow — must not be a wide swing
- Elbow safety protocol: every elbow round uses elbow pads in drilling until coach removes restriction

**Performance standards:**
- Horizontal elbow: 20 clean reps on elbow pad (no forearm contact)
- Elbow + teep combination: link elbow with range creation in drilling round
- Coach verbal confirmation: "safe to use in controlled sparring" — required before this level is awarded

**Session requirement:** 56+ classes
**Character standard:** composure and control in drilling; no wild or angry elbows
**Coach criteria:** elbow technique is safe; athlete understands range and consequence

---

## TIER 3 — THE CLINCH
*"The clinch is where Muay Thai is won and lost. Most Western fighters never learn it. You will."*
Armband: dark oxblood/red cotton — two marks added over three levels

---

### TIER 3 / LEVEL 1 — CLINCH ENTRY & CONTROL
**Identity:** You can get inside and stay inside. Posture is maintained.

**Technical requirements:**
- Clinch entry: from punching range, enter with arm under-then-over (swim-in), establish double collar tie
- Posture in the clinch: head forward and down (not upright), arms connected, elbows tight
- Hand fighting in the clinch: break opponent's grip, re-establish your own — pummeling in clinch
- Control drill: maintain double collar tie position for 2 minutes vs. partner attempting to break
- Exit the clinch: push opponent off with forearms, create distance, re-establish stance

**Performance standards:**
- Maintain clinch control position (double collar tie) for a full 3-minute drilling round
- Break and re-establish grip 5 times when opponent fights for underhook

**Session requirement:** 64+ classes
**Coach criteria:** clinch posture correct; hands are active, not passive

---

### TIER 3 / LEVEL 2 — KNEES & SWEEPS IN THE CLINCH
**Identity:** You score from the clinch. Opponents don't want to be there with you.

**Technical requirements:**
- Knee to body from double collar tie: pull opponent's posture down into knee, 10 clean reps
- Knee to head: opponent bent over — controlled, coached scenario only
- Long knee (straight knee): extend and drive, opponent's advancing — timing-based
- Sweep (foot sweep from clinch): opponent's weight forward, inner-foot trip, finish to mat
- Sweep (hip throw from clinch): kouchi-adjacent, opponent's off-balance moment, off-hip throw
- Combination: knee → exit → teep → re-enter → knee

**Performance standards:**
- Score 3 knees in a controlled clinch round (partner not defending hard)
- Execute sweep and land partner safely to mat in drilling (mat safety protocol required)

**Session requirement:** 72+ classes
**Coach criteria:** knees connect because of pull — not because of pure power; sweeps are technical

---

### TIER 3 / LEVEL 3 — DIRTY BOXING & EXITS
**Identity:** You are dangerous from mid-range to close range to the clinch.

**Technical requirements:**
- Dirty boxing in the clinch: short hooks, short uppercuts — elbows tight, power from hip
- Frame and push: mid-clinch frame (forearm on neck or chest), push opponent back to kicking range
- Grab-and-knee off a missed punch: opponent swings and misses, grab arm, pull into knee
- Exit to round kick: clinch exit creates angle, round kick immediately to open side

**Performance standards:**
- Link entry → knee → exit → kick in a combination during padwork

**Session requirement:** 80+ classes
**Coach criteria:** mid-range is not dead space — athlete fills it

---

## TIER 4 — COMBINATIONS & TIMING
*"Now you can fight. What does your fight look like?"*
Armband: black cotton — two marks added over three levels

---

### TIER 4 / LEVEL 1 — COMBINATION ARCHITECTURE
**Identity:** You throw combinations with purpose — not just sequences. Every combination is built to end somewhere specific.

**Technical requirements:**
- 4-weapon combination: jab → cross → round kick body → teep (rhythm, no pausing)
- Body-head combination: body shot (hook or kick) → follow immediately high (cross or elbow)
- Defensive combination: block → counter → follow — not just defense, but offense launched off defense
- Pad round without prompting: athlete calls the combinations during a pad round (not coach calling)
- Combination ending positions: know where you are (in range, out of range, in clinch) after every combo

**Session requirement:** 92+ classes
**Performance standards:**
- Coach observes 3-minute pad round and identifies 3 intentional combination structures (not accidents)
**Coach criteria:** combinations have architecture — there is a reason for the first strike and a target for the last

---

### TIER 4 / LEVEL 2 — COUNTER FIGHTING
**Identity:** You are most dangerous when you are hit first. Counter fighting is your reflex.

**Technical requirements:**
- Catch-and-counter: catch incoming kick with arms, immediately return kick or enter clinch
- Slip-and-cross: slip outside incoming jab, rear cross counter
- Parry-and-enter: parry opponent's cross, step in, enter clinch or throw short elbow
- Pull-and-counter: pull back (lean back) off straight punch, return 1-2 to open face
- 3-count counter drill: partner throws 3 weapons, athlete defends all 3, counter on #3

**Session requirement:** 100+ classes
**Performance standards:**
- Land 5 of 10 counter combinations in 3-minute controlled sparring round
**Coach criteria:** athlete is harder to hit when moving backward than when standing still

---

### TIER 4 / LEVEL 3 — RING IQ
**Identity:** You understand the ring. You know when you're in trouble before the coach sees it.

**Technical requirements:**
- Ring cutting: how to cut off an opponent moving laterally; footwork pattern demonstrated
- Pressure fighting vs. outfighting: athlete identifies which they are and can demonstrate the opposite
- Pace variation: hard 30 seconds → technical 30 seconds → hard 30 seconds — coach confirms it's visible
- Dominant side targeting: can identify opponent's dominant side and target the weaker side

**Session requirement:** 112+ classes
**Performance standards:**
- Pad session where athlete calls pace variations unprompted
**Coach criteria:** ring IQ is a coach conversation, not a checklist — this level is awarded based on a coaching discussion, not a skills test

---

## TIER 5 — FIGHT MIND
*"This is not about winning. It is about who you become when it matters most."*
Armband: black and brass double-wrapped — the complete Prajioud

---

### TIER 5 / LEVEL 1 — CONTROLLED SPARRING
**Identity:** You can spar with composure. Fear does not become aggression. Aggression does not become wildness.

**Technical requirements:**
- Controlled sparring: 10+ rounds of technical sparring (partner not trying to hurt each other — technique-focused)
- Composure under pressure: when hit hard, athlete slows down (not speeds up)
- Defense in sparring: coach watches a full round and counts clean shots landed — athlete passes if count is appropriate for level
- Energy management: 3-round spar without visible gassing

**Session requirement:** 128+ classes + 10 documented sparring rounds
**Coach criteria:** this athlete is safe to spar with; partners trust them

---

### TIER 5 / LEVEL 2 — FIGHT STRATEGY
**Identity:** Every round you fight has a plan. Every adjustment is visible to the coach.

**Technical requirements:**
- Pre-spar game plan: athlete states a game plan before each sparring round (opponent-specific or focus-specific)
- Mid-round adjustment: coach calls a mid-round adjustment; athlete demonstrates it in the same round
- Post-round breakdown: after the round, athlete identifies 2 things that worked and 1 thing to change

**Session requirement:** 144+ classes
**Coach criteria:** the athlete's rounds are improving because of thinking, not just because of conditioning

---

### TIER 5 / LEVEL 3 — COMPLETE NAK MUAY
**Identity:** You are a complete Muay Thai fighter. All eight limbs are operational. You are eligible to be called a practitioner of the art.

**Requirements:**
- All Tier 1-4 techniques demonstrable on command
- Coach verbal confirmation: "This athlete can be trusted to demonstrate Muay Thai correctly to anyone who asks"
- 5-round capacity: complete 5 rounds (3 minutes each) at technical sparring pace — technique maintained throughout
- Character: teaches newer practitioners when asked; models composure; embodies Thai fight culture (respect, humility, controlled aggression)

**Session requirement:** 160+ classes + 25+ sparring rounds
**Eligible for:** Instructor track (separate evaluation process)
**Coach criteria:** the athlete represents the gym well on any floor in any gym

# PART 4 — BOXING
## "Sweet science. Sharp mind. Strong heart."

Boxing is not about power. It never was. The best boxers in history talked about the sport the way chess players talk about chess — with patience, precision, and deep respect for the geometry of two people trying to hit each other without being hit. The Cage Logic Technician System maps that development from the first stance to the complete Sweet Science practitioner.

**6 Tiers. No shortcuts. No punching harder to get through a level you don't understand yet.**

---

### FOUNDATION
**Identity:** You can stand correctly and your jab has a snap.
**What this phase is:** Before combinations, before sparring, before anything — stand correctly and throw a jab that means something.

**Technical requirements:**
- Orthodox or southpaw stance: correct foot alignment, weight distribution (60/40 front/rear), guard position
- Guard: gloves at cheekbone height, elbows protecting ribs, chin down
- Jab mechanics: shoulder roll, full extension, snap back — glove returns to same position it left
- Footwork: step-jab (advance with jab), step-back (retreat), left pivot, right pivot
- Breathing: exhale on contact — consistent throughout pad round

**Performance standards:**
- Correct stance held for entire pad round without coach correction
- 50 jabs thrown on bag — all snapping back to guard

**Session requirement:** 16+ classes
**Coach criteria:** the jab is a weapon, not a tap; stance is automatic at rest

---

### TECHNICIAN LEVEL 1
**Identity:** Your 1-2 is automatic. You move without thinking about moving.

**Technical requirements:**
- 1-2 combination (jab-cross): weight transfer on cross, hip rotation, shoulder drives through, guard restored after
- Footwork with combination: step-jab or jab-in-place, pivot off cross to 45° angle
- Basic head movement: bob (bend knees), weave (lateral shoulder dip under hook)
- Jab accuracy: 70%+ on focus mitts in 3-minute round
- Hook mechanics (introduction): short hook (bent elbow, horizontal fist rotation, pivot on lead foot) — drilling only, not yet in combinations

**Performance standards:**
- 1-2 to pads: coach calls "snap and return" — must be confirmed 5 of 5 reps
- Footwork drill: step-jab, pivot, step-back — continuous for 2 minutes without error

**Session requirement:** 24+ classes
**Conditioning:** 3 rounds of 3 minutes on the heavy bag — footwork maintained throughout
**Coach criteria:** 1-2 is a reflex; head movement is beginning to appear off the back of combination

---

### TECHNICIAN LEVEL 2
**Identity:** Your defense is as dangerous as your offense. Slipping a punch creates a counter.

**Technical requirements:**
- Left hook: correct elbow angle (90°), pivot on lead foot, hip rotation, fist horizontal at point of contact
- Right uppercut: bend knees, drop slight, drive up through hips and shoulder — not a wide swing
- Left uppercut: same mechanics on opposite side
- Slip outside (off jab): move outside opponent's jab, right cross counter follows automatically
- Slip inside (off jab): move inside opponent's jab, left hook counter
- Pull-counter: pull back off straight, return 1-2
- Roll under hook: shoulder dip and roll under incoming hook, return short hook or uppercut
- Body work: left hook to body, right cross to body — keep guard maintained throughout

**Performance standards:**
- Complete 4-weapon combination (jab-cross-hook-cross) on pads at speed
- Slip and counter: time 3 of 5 slips correctly in partner drilling (partner throws at 50% speed)

**Session requirement:** 36+ classes
**Conditioning:** 4 rounds, 3 minutes each — head movement present throughout
**Coach criteria:** defense is not a block; it creates positions for offense — this must be visible

---

### TECHNICIAN LEVEL 3
**Identity:** Combinations flow. You are fighting in sequences, not punches.

**Technical requirements:**
- 3-5 punch combinations: minimum 5 different 3-punch or longer combinations drilled and reproducible
- Signature combination: athlete identifies one 3+ punch combination that is theirs — their rhythm, their flow
- Counter timing: wait for the third punch in an opponent's combination, counter on the opening
- Jab accuracy in combination: jab that starts a combination must land — combinations thrown off missed jabs are habits to break
- Ring control: cutting off the ring — footwork pattern to corner or rope an opponent against a wall (drilling concept)
- Body-to-head: start low (body jab, body cross), end high (hook or uppercut to head)
- High-low feint: feint high, throw low; feint low, throw high

**Performance standards:**
- 6 of 9 Level 3 coach-set requirements met (per mockup standard)
- Pad round without prompting: athlete calls combinations
- Jab accuracy 70%+ in a live round against a moving target

**Session requirement:** 48+ classes
**Conditioning:** 5 rounds, 3 minutes, 1-minute rest — output maintained throughout (no fade)
**Coach criteria:** this athlete can be trusted in a controlled sparring environment

---

### FIGHTER LEVEL 4
**Identity:** You can spar. You can think. You can take a punch and come back with something better.

**Technical requirements:**
- 20 documented sparring rounds (3-minute rounds, technical/controlled pace)
- Pressure fighting: cut off the ring, work behind jab, force opponent to corners and ropes
- Outfighting: maintain range with jab, use lateral footwork, land and move, avoid exchanges
- Athlete identifies which style they are (pressure or outfighter) — coach confirms the identification is correct
- Body shot damage: 3 rounds where athlete specifically targets the body in sparring — coach confirms intentional body work visible
- Jab accuracy in sparring: 60%+ over 3-minute round (coach tracked)
- Defense success: can take a hard shot without panicking — composure immediately after being hit

**Performance standards:**
- 6 of 9 requirements met (matching mockup standard for Technician 3 → Fighter 4 threshold)
- Coach verbal: "This athlete is safe to spar at this gym with any compatible-level partner"

**Session requirement:** 72+ classes + 20 sparring rounds
**Competition or equivalent:** 1 amateur bout OR 15 hard sparring rounds against more advanced practitioners
**Conditioning:** 6 rounds, 3 minutes each, at sparring pace — no fade, no quit
**Coach criteria:** the athlete thinks in the round; adjustments visible between rounds

---

### ELITE LEVEL 5
**Identity:** The Sweet Science. Complete.
**What this means:** Not just technically proficient. A student of the sport. Someone who can watch a round and know what is happening and why. Someone a beginner should stand next to and watch.

**Requirements:**
- All Technician Level 1-4 techniques demonstrable on command
- Ring generalship: coach watches 3 sparring rounds and identifies at least 2 visible examples of ring generalship (cutting off, controlling pace, feinting, making opponent fight your fight)
- Opponent-specific adjustment: coach assigns an opponent with a visible pattern; athlete makes a specific adjustment; adjustment works
- Fight IQ: can watch a round between two boxers and accurately describe what each fighter is doing strategically (verbal debrief with coach)
- Teaching: demonstrate and correctly teach any technique from Levels 1-3 when asked

**Session requirement:** 96+ classes + 30+ sparring rounds
**Character standard:** composure is the constant — in sparring, in teaching, in setbacks
**Coach criteria:** the elite level is a coach conversation, not a skills test. The coach must be able to say: "This person embodies the Sweet Science. Not just executes it."

# PART 5 — MMA
## "Integrate. Adapt. Dominate."

MMA is not a discipline. It is the test of all disciplines. The progression system reflects that — you do not advance by learning new techniques. You advance by proving you can use what you know across contexts, under pressure, against resistance.

The visual: the Skill Integration Hexagon. Six connections between disciplines (striking-to-takedown, takedown-to-top-control, top-control-to-submissions, submissions-to-scrambles, scrambles-to-feet, feet-to-striking). As the athlete advances, the connections light up. Foundation has no lit connections. Elite has all six fully active.

**Six stages. Each one proves the integration runs deeper.**

---

### STAGE 1 — FOUNDATION
**Identity:** "You can move between worlds without stopping to think about it."
**The test:** Strike → clinch → takedown entry. One seamless motion. No pause at the transition.

**Technical requirements:**

*Striking in MMA context:*
- Orthodox/southpaw stance switch: reactive (based on opponent's stance, not pre-set)
- Jab as range finder and shot setter: jab creates head reaction, level change follows
- Clinch entry off combination: 1-2 → shoot, or 1 → grab, or kick → clinch
- Defensive striking: cover-and-shoot (take a punch and immediately shoot for takedown)

*Wrestling in MMA context:*
- Single leg and double leg: standard techniques, now with punch defense on the entry
- Cage/wall work: cage entry, cage takedown (trip + drive), cage control for 30 seconds
- Takedown defense: sprawl off jab-fake → level change; re-establish striking range
- Mat returns: after scramble both to standing, re-engage in clinch, complete takedown

*Ground and pound:*
- Posture in top control: head up, hips loaded, hammer fists and crossed-arm shots
- Ground and pound safety: controlled contact in drilling; not a power session — positioning and mechanics
- Pass to ground and pound: from side control → mount transition → continue striking
- Get-up under pressure: tactical standup from bottom position while avoiding ground and pound

*Submission in MMA:*
- Rear naked choke: from back position in MMA context (opponent defending with elbows and posture)
- Triangle and armbar from guard: guard in MMA context — opponent punching into guard
- Guillotine from standing: shot defense → front headlock → guillotine entry

**Performance standards:**
- Strike-to-clinch-to-takedown: complete the chain in one motion (drilling, then live)
- Survive 5 live MMA rounds (light contact, technical pace) — no positions cause panic
- Coach can identify a basic game plan in athlete's rolling after watching 1 round

**Session requirement:** 36+ MMA-specific classes
**Conditioning:** 5 rounds, 5 minutes each, technical pace — maintain technique in final round
**Coach criteria:** athlete does not freeze at the transitions between striking and grappling

---

### STAGE 2 — AMATEUR READY
**Identity:** "You can compete at amateur level without embarrassing yourself or getting hurt."
**The test:** 5-minute round against an amateur-level opponent. Coach observes and confirms: "This athlete is ready."

**Technical requirements:**

*Integration proofs:*
- Demonstrate all takedown entries (minimum 12 documented attempts across sessions)
- Submit from 3 different positions using 3 different submissions (not the same submission from the same place)
- Score 3+ clean ground and pound moments in a live round (coach tracked)
- Complete Strength & Power program at Foundation level (4 of 6 units)

*Striking development:*
- Combinations in combination: throw, clinch, break out, throw again — no reset to neutral stance between exchanges
- Body targeting: body kick, body knee, ground-and-pound to body — all present and intentional

*Wrestling development:*
- Takedown off defense: sprawl → re-shot immediately (no reset); demonstrate 3 times
- Cage control: pin opponent to cage, execute trip takedown, maintain top position

*Grappling development:*
- Escape from bad positions: bottom mount, bottom back-take (back-taken on you), bottom side control — escape route from all three

**Performance standards:**
- Fight IQ assessment (coach verbal): athlete can describe what they want before the round
- Official sparring assessment: 1 observed round with coach evaluation form completed

**Session requirement:** 60+ MMA sessions
**Competition or equivalent:** 1 amateur bout OR 20 hard rounds against equivalent athletes
**Coach criteria:** physically, technically, and mentally ready to compete safely at amateur level

---

### STAGE 3 — CAMP READY
**Identity:** "Your system holds under real pressure. 3 weeks out from a fight, you're ready to train camp."
**The test:** 3 rounds of hard sparring. Output does not drop in round 3.

**Technical requirements:**

*System integration:*
- Skill Integration Map: coach evaluates all 6 connections — at least 4 must be operational under pressure
- Dominant weapon identified: coach can name athlete's primary weapon without hesitation
- Defensive system identified: athlete has a go-to defensive posture in every scenario

*Advanced wrestling:*
- Chain takedown: attempt + 2 follow-up attacks in one sequence during live MMA sparring
- Cage to mat: multiple cage takedown entries that work against active resistance

*Advanced striking:*
- Setups: feint leading to real attack — coach can identify intentional feints in sparring (not accidents)
- Body-head combination in live sparring: body shot opens the head, athlete executes, coach confirms

*Advanced grappling:*
- Leg lock awareness in MMA: recognizes ashi position, disengages or finishes (no stuck position)
- Advanced submissions: athlete has one submission beyond the basics that works in live rolling (d'arce, kimura, body triangle, for example)

*Fight Readiness Tracker:*
- Strength: 7.0/10 minimum (per Cage Logic training load tracking)
- Endurance: 7.0/10 minimum
- Recovery: maintained above 6.5 consistently across camp period
- Mental Focus: psych module baseline established (BRS score logged)

**Session requirement:** 90+ MMA sessions
**Competition or equivalent:** 3+ bouts or 40+ hard training rounds
**Conditioning:** 2 consecutive 5-minute rounds at full pace — technique maintained throughout both
**Coach criteria:** athlete is a training asset for the room — makes partners better by being present

---

### STAGE 4 — COMPETITION READY
**Identity:** "Consistent, dangerous, coachable. You're a fighter."

**Technical requirements:**
- All 6 Integration Hexagon connections operational — coach confirms each under live conditions
- Gameplan execution: given a specific gameplan, athlete executes recognizable version of it in sparring
- Adjustment between rounds: visible adjustment made between rounds in a 3-round session
- Signature system: athlete has a primary chain (strike → takedown → position → finish) that is uniquely theirs
- Opponent reading: watch 2 rounds of a training partner, identify their pattern, adjust game plan, demonstrate the adjustment

*Physical standards:*
- Strength: 7.5/10
- Endurance: 7.5/10
- Recovery: consistent above 7.0
- Mental Focus: BRS score 20+ (post-loss resilience present)

**Session requirement:** 120+ MMA sessions
**Competition record:** 5+ bouts with consistent results (not required to be undefeated; results are less important than growth)
**Coach criteria:** athlete receives coaching between rounds and the coach sees it in the next round — coachability is the test

---

### STAGE 5 — PRO READY
**Identity:** "Fight IQ, pacing, composure — all present. You belong at the professional level."

**Technical requirements:**
- 5-round capacity: complete 5 consecutive 5-minute rounds at near-competitive pace — technique and output maintained in round 5
- Signature chain: defined, tested, and working against high-level training partners
- Defensive system complete: no position creates panic; all major defensive scenarios have a trained response
- Post-loss performance: if athlete has lost a bout, demonstrate the specific adjustment made in next 3 sessions (documented)
- Scouting integration: study 3 rounds of film (or live observation) on a training partner; produce a verbal gameplan; execute recognizable version

*Physical standards:*
- Strength: 8.0/10
- Endurance: 8.0/10
- Recovery: consistent above 7.5
- Mental Focus: BRS score 24+; completed post-loss reflection module (if applicable)

**Session requirement:** 160+ MMA sessions
**Competition record:** enough fights and rounds to have genuine experience; quality over quantity
**Coach criteria:** the coach has run out of easy things to correct; the work is now refinement, not installation

---

### STAGE 6 — ELITE
**Identity:** "You are the problem."
**What this means:** Coaches bring other athletes over to watch you. Sparring partners leave rounds more educated. Your game has a name. Other fighters in the region know it.

**Requirements:**

# APPENDIX A — THE 90-DAY INFLECTION POINT

Every discipline is designed around one shared insight from the Iron Army BJJ program:

> "Weeks 5–8: The wall. You've been training long enough to feel bad about your progress but not long enough to feel good about it. Most people quit here."

In every discipline, the weeks 5–8 period has the largest and most specific small wins. This is intentional. The app surfaces them exactly when the athlete's motivation is at its lowest. That is the whole product. Progress is invisible without a map. The map is the margin.

---

# APPENDIX B — COACH VERIFICATION STANDARDS

Every tier advancement in every discipline requires coach signoff. This is non-negotiable across all five sports. The app tracks everything automatically — sessions, metrics, completion rates. But the tier does not advance until a human authority confirms it.

This mirrors how these sports actually work. No badge, no checklist, no algorithm promotes an athlete. A coach does. The app documents the evidence. The coach makes the call.

Badge language across all disciplines: **COACH VERIFIED** — not Approved, not Certified, not Unlocked.

## The idea

Make it easy for a practitioner to **share their own score** outward — a clean, factual score card they can post to social media (Instagram, etc.). Every athlete who posts theirs is free reach for Iron Army and Cage Logic.

## The line that keeps it on-brand

The charter is locked: "no leaderboards, no celebration of mediocrity, mirror not megaphone." A share feature has an on-brand version and an off-brand version, and the difference is sharp:

- **On-brand — do this:** the practitioner shares *their own* mirror outward. A dry, factual score card they choose to post. Self-owned reflection, not the app shouting. Still "mirror, not megaphone."
- **Off-brand — the charter wall:** in-app leaderboards ranking users against each other, auto-celebrations, streak-flexing. Don't build this.

**The rule:** share your own score outward = fine. Rank users against each other inside the app = no.

## Guardrails it must clear

1. **Opt-in, user-owned.** Athletes own their data (charter). Sharing is explicit and user-controlled — never automatic, never default-on.
2. **Voice.** The share card stays dry and factual — no "crushing it," no confetti, no exclamation marks. Chris owns the card's voice/design.
3. **Privacy.** No partner names, no other athletes' data on a shared card without consent. Encryption-sensitive fields never surface.
